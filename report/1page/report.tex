\section*{Problem Context}

Tropical cyclone (TC) forecasting in data-scarce ocean basins is limited by small
training sets. The South Pacific (SP) contains 5$\times$ fewer storms than the
Western Pacific (WP) in the TropiCycloneNet dataset (30 vs.\ 131 storms),
making it impractical to train reliable models on SP alone~[1].
Transfer learning from data-rich to data-scarce basins is a natural solution,
yet a fundamental physical barrier exists: the Coriolis force reverses across
hemispheres, causing WP storms to track NW/W while SP storms track SE/W/SW.

Despite this directional shift, the underlying atmospheric physics governing TC
intensity, sea-surface temperature (SST) and vertical wind shear, is shared
across basins~[3]. A physics-aware model that disentangles
hemisphere-dependent kinematics from universal thermodynamics should therefore
transfer effectively with minimal adaptation. We encode the Coriolis reversal
through a phase shift on directional labels, following evidence that
WP-only models fail on Southern Hemisphere basins without hemisphere-aware
preprocessing~[1].

\section*{Proposed Approach}

We evaluate \textbf{five architectures} on zero-shot WP$\to$SP transfer,
then fine-tune the best performers:

\vspace{-2pt}
{\footnotesize
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}lX@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Model} & \textbf{Transfer hypothesis} \\
\midrule
U-Net & Spatial baseline; strong 3D field reconstruction \\
ResNet & Deep residual features; proven image classifier \\
FNO & Mesh-invariant; spectral basin-agnostic maps \\
PI-GAN & Adversarial + vorticity/PV conservation \\
Transformer & Cross-attention fusion of 3 modalities \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
}
\vspace{2pt}

\textbf{Three-stage pipeline:}
\begin{enumerate}[nosep, leftmargin=1.2em, label=\arabic*.]
  \item \textbf{Zero-shot}: Train on WP, apply Coriolis phase shift, evaluate on SP.
  \item \textbf{Fine-tune}: Adapt top models on 10/25/50\% of SP data; measure accuracy vs.\ compute.
  \item \textbf{Extend}: Test generalization to NI and SI basins.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Dataset}

TropiCycloneNet~[1] covers 6 ocean basins (1950--2023) with three modalities
at 6-hourly resolution: \textbf{Data1D} (cyclone-center time series from
IBTrACS~[5]), \textbf{Data3D} (81$\times$81 gridded SST, u/v wind,
geopotential at 0.25\textdegree), and \textbf{Env-Data} (structured
environmental features).

\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
& \textbf{WP} & \textbf{SP} & \textbf{Ratio} \\
\midrule
Storms & 131 & 30 & 4.4$\times$ \\
Timesteps & 4{,}562 & 922 & 5.0$\times$ \\
Top directions & NW/W & SE/W & Mirrored \\
Mean shear & 8.2\,m/s & 7.4\,m/s & Similar \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}


Wind shear and SST distributions overlap substantially between WP and SP
(Fig.~4), confirming that universal intensity predictors~[3]
should transfer. Direction distributions are hemisphere-mirrored (Fig.~3),
validating our Coriolis phase-shift preprocessing.
